About

Santo Romano is an accomplished educator, graphic designer, front-end developer and musician. He has over 20 years of teaching experience at Concordia University in the department of Design and Computation Arts. He has produced award-winning websites and apps for some of the largest pharmaceutical companies in North America like Pfizer, Aventis and Merck Frost. As well, he has acted as lead site architect and programmer for the ground breaking Continuing Medical Education website mypatient.com for I.C. Axon and the University of Virginia. He holds a BA in History from Concordia University and specializes in Graphic Design and UX. His pioneering work in the field of online education, "StudioWeb.com" has been adopted by 1500 schools in North America and is helping to educate the next generation of web programmers.

President's Excellence in Teaching Award

read more

Santo is one of the few exceptional professors at Concordia University to be honored with the President's Excellence in Teaching Award. With a passion for inspiring students and creating an inclusive learning environment, Santo stands out as a dedicated educator. Through effective communication and innovative instructional strategies, Santo strives to foster critical thinking skills and empower students to actively engage in their own learning journey. With this recognition, Santo's commitment to excellence in teaching was acknowledged and celebrated.

Teaching Philosophy

Santo employs an educational philosophy that encompasses a set of guiding principles and beliefs that shape his approach to teaching and learning. It emphasizes the holistic development of learners, focusing not only on academic knowledge but also on fostering critical thinking, creativity, and social-emotional skills.

He recognizes the uniqueness of each student and strives to create inclusive and learner-centered environments that cater to diverse learning styles and needs. He promotes active and experiential learning, encouraging students to engage in hands-on activities, problem-solving, and collaboration.

The philosophy values lifelong learning, emphasizing the importance of curiosity, adaptability, and continuous growth for both students and educators. He aims to cultivate well-rounded individuals who are prepared for the challenges of the modern world and equipped with the skills and knowledge needed to contribute positively to society.

Open Borders Poster

Rise Up.net, a grassroots organization dedicated to social justice and equality, requested a protest poster for a demonstration at the US/Quebec Lacolle border crossing, advocating for open borders and opposing racism. Recognizing the significance of the cause, Santo eagerly accepted the opportunity to lend his artistic talent to promote such a vital message. The design captured the essence of unity and compassion but also highlighted the urgent need to combat racism and discrimination. Through his artistic expression, Santo aimed to evoke empathy, incite dialogue, and ultimately inspire positive change in society. The resulting poster would serve as a rallying cry against the divisive forces that sought to divide us and emphasize the profound importance of embracing diversity and inclusivity in our world.
